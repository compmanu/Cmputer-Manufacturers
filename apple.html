Apple Inc.

Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. Apple was founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne on April 1, 1976, to develop and sell personal computers. Today, Apple is known for its innovative products such as the iPhone, iPad, Mac computers, and Apple Watch.

Apple's first product, the Apple I, was a hand-built computer sold as a motherboard (with CPU, RAM, and basic textual-video chips) to hobbyists. The Apple II, released in 1977, was one of the first successful mass-produced microcomputers. Apple's introduction of the Macintosh in 1984 marked a milestone with its graphical user interface, setting the standard for personal computing.
